# Executive Office of the President (EOP)

- under the [[White House office]]
- created by FDR
	- drastically increased the size of the executive branch
- "cluster of staff agencies that help the president carry out their responsibilities"
- no constant contact with the president even though they report to the president
- appointments must be confirmed by the Senate
	- 2/3 vote
- includes
	- Office of Management and Budget (OMB)
		- oversees the development and implementation of the president's ideas throughout the executive branch
		- budget development and management
		- creation of legislative proposals
		- deals with implementing executive orders
	- Central Intelligence Agency (CIA)
		- foreign intelligence
		- contributes significantly to national security
		- covert action
		- created by the National Security Act of 1947
	- Council of Economic Advisers (CEA)
		- provides the president with objective economic information
		- influences domestic and international policies
		- synthesizes economic research
	- Office of Personnel Management (OPM)
		- human resources
		- manages federal healthcare and retirement benefits
		- inclusive hiring
	- US Trade Representative (USTR)
		- works toward open markets
		- coordinates international trade
		- created the USMCA
			- US-Mexico-Canada Agreement
